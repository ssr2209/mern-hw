Neymar Jr is a Brazilian professional footballer who plays as a forward for Al Hilal and the Brazil national team. Known for his flair, creativity, and technical brilliance, Neymar is one of the most popular athletes in the world.

Early life

Neymar da Silva Santos Júnior was born on February 5, 1992, in Mogi das Cruzes, São Paulo, Brazil. He grew up in a modest family and showed great promise in futsal and street football as a child. His father, a former footballer, became his mentor and agent. Neymar joined Santos FC's youth academy at age 11 and rapidly became a star in Brazil by his mid-teens.

Club career

Santos FC (2009–2013): Debuted professionally at 17. Scored 136 goals in 225 matches. Won Copa Libertadores (2011) and multiple domestic titles. FC Barcelona (2013–2017): Formed the iconic attacking trio “MSN” with Messi and Suárez. Scored 105 goals in 186 games. Major Titles: 2 La Liga, 3 Copa del Rey, 1 UEFA Champions League (2015) Paris Saint-Germain (2017–2023): Transferred for a world-record €222M. Scored 118 goals in 173 appearances. Won multiple Ligue 1 titles, and reached the UCL final in 2020. Al-Hilal (2023–Present): Signed with the Saudi club in 2023 for a high-profile deal but suffered long-term injuries early in his stint. Career Club Stats (as of mid-2025): Total Club Goals: 300+ Total Appearances: 600+

International career

Neymar debuted for Brazil's senior team in 2010. Scored 80+ goals, becoming one of Brazil's top all-time scorers, surpassing Pelé. Major Achievements: Olympic Gold Medal (2016, Rio) FIFA Confederations Cup (2013) Copa América runner-up (2021, missed 2019 win due to injury) Represented Brazil in 3 World Cups (2014, 2018, 2022), known for flair and emotion on the world stage.

Style of play

Neymar is recognized for: Dribbling, flair, and creativity. Often plays as a left winger or attacking midfielder. Known for trickery, close control, and showmanship. Excellent at creating chances, drawing fouls, and scoring from tight angles. Criticized at times for diving theatrics, but undeniably brilliant in attack.

Personal life

Family: Very close to his father and sister Rafaella. Has a son, Davi Lucca, born in 2011. A global celebrity, Neymar is active on social media and has sponsorships with Puma, Red Bull, and others. Enjoys music, gaming, and fashion off the field. Involved in charity work through the Instituto Neymar Jr., helping underprivileged children in Brazil. Despite injuries and controversies, he remains one of football’s most marketable and skillful talents.
